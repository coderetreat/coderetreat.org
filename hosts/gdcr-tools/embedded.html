On this page, we want to provide you with some handy tools for your event.

Please bring a proper camera (and if possible audio setup) to your event. Part of GDCR is having a couple quick video calls (skype, google hangout, etc) with other cities to say hello.

It can be fun to have a call at the start of the day with a city that is just ending. That way, you can pass the “global baton” around the world.

When doing a call, make sure it is fast. Just a quick “Hello from {your city}! Have a great day!”. Too long of a call can be awkward. :)

Like in the previous years, we have setup Video Booths so you can visit other locations via webcam.

Coordinating:

  • We recommend you stay in your video booth all day long. Even if you only have short chats with other locations (recommended), staying in the video booth will make it a lot easier to coordinate the chats. It is also fun to watch what is happening in the other locations throughout the day.
  • Project the video booth all day on a dedicated screen or projector.
  • If no one is in your video booth, then you can use the #gdrc channel set up on the Software Craftsmanship Slack Community to see if anyone is available and interested to have a quick call.
  • Use Twitter to find locations running their coderetreat around the same time as yours and who may be available for a video chat.
  • If there is no one in your video booth, then feel free to join a different video booth...

You can find more information about facilitation in this page: Facilitating a Coderetreat.

If you have any questions, please find us in the #coderetreat channel on the Software Crafters Slack.
